My Story

Why I started
Helping Hand for NRI.

My name is Bharathi, from Tiruppur. I am a single son to my mother, and I have spent years sailing worldwide on ships.

The one thing that always worried me while I was at sea was my mother — alone at home. That worry came true when she fell in a temple and broke her arm. She didn't tell me for days, not wanting me to worry. By the time I found out, my contract had ended and I came home.

There was also the time I was building our house. I had to go back to the ship mid-construction, leaving my mother alone to deal with the contractor — following up on small things, making decisions, handling issues she shouldn't have had to face alone. It was stressful for her and heartbreaking for me to watch from so far away.

Two years later, it happened again. She fell down the stairs and hit her head. I only found out during a video call when I noticed something was wrong and asked. She had been alone, and hadn't told me.

"This really broke me."

When I came home, I decided to stay. While thinking about what I could do, I told a friend of mine who is an NRI in the US — if he ever needs any help back home, I will do it for him. He appreciated it and encouraged me to turn it into a proper service for NRIs like him.

And here we are. Helping Hand for NRI was born from my own pain — so you never have to feel the helplessness I felt. 🙏
